## **Project Overview** 

The Final Project is the capstone evaluation for this course. It combines a **group project** , a **group presentation** , and an **individual viva** into a single 35-mark component. Each group of **8 students** (assigned randomly) works on **two tasks** : one end-to-end applied project and one focused conceptual/experimental task. 

## **Two Tasks Per Group** 

**Task 1 (Section 1):** A small, interesting end-to-end project - build something real, train it, evaluate it. This builds **confidence** and hands-on skills. 

**Task 2 (Section 2):** A focused experimental task designed to build **theoretical and conceptual foundations** - understand _why_ things work, not just _how_ to make them run.

## **Group Formation & Contribution Policy** 

-  Groups of **8 students** , assigned **randomly** 

-  Each group picks (or is assigned) **one topic from Section 1** and **one topic from Section 2** 

## **Vote-Out Policy for Non-Contributing Members** 

If a group member is **not making any contributions** , the rest of the group can **vote them out** (requires majority vote + evidence submitted to the TA). 

## **For voted-out students:** 

-  No penalty for leaving the group 

-  They must complete the **same topic** as an **individual project** 

-  Same deadlines, same rubric - just solo 

-  This is meant as a safety net, not a punishment - but the workload is real 

## **Why Random Viva Selection?** 

Since one person’s viva determines 30% of the group’s marks, **every member** has an incentive to ensure that **every other member** understands the project deeply. This creates natural peer teaching and shared accountability. The announcement is made one day before so the group can do a final prep session together.

## **Viva Format** 

-  **One person** from each group is chosen **randomly** , announced **one day before** 

-  The viva lasts **8-10 minutes** : project questions + theory questions 

-  The evaluator picks from the theory question bank (shared with students in advance) 

-  The viva score of this one member becomes the **viva score for the entire group** 

-  This means: if your group’s representative scores 4/11, **everyone** in the group gets 4/11 

## **Viva Scoring Rubric** 

- **9-11:** Explains confidently, handles follow-ups, connects theory to their project decisions 

- **6-8:** Good understanding, minor gaps on follow-up questions 

- **3-5:** Surface-level understanding, cannot explain why certain decisions were made 

- **0-2:** Cannot explain the project or answer basic theory questions
